What is Budgeting?

A budget is a written plan that allocates your expected income towards expenses, savings, and debt repayment over a set period — usually one month.

The popular 50/30/20 rule, described in Elizabeth Warren's All Your Worth, suggests spending 50% on needs, 30% on wants, and saving 20%.

How to Get Started

Step-by-Step Plan

  1. List all sources of monthly income
  2. Track every expense — no matter how small
    • Food & Groceries
    • Transport & Fuel
    • Entertainment
    • Subscriptions
  3. Calculate your net balance
  4. Set a savings target and automate transfers
  5. Review and adjust every month

💡 Top Money Tips

Save First

Pay yourself first — set aside at least 10% of every pay cheque before spending anything.

Review Weekly

Spend 10 minutes every Sunday reviewing your week's expenses. Awareness is the first step to change.

Emergency Fund

Build 3–6 months of living costs in a liquid savings account before investing elsewhere.

✂ Cut Subscriptions

List every subscription. Cancel anything unused in 30 days — savings add up fast.
